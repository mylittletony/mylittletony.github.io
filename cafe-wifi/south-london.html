South London Venues

Peckham Refreshment Rooms

(12-16 Blenheim Grove SE15 4Q)

Poignant in the freelance community, it has a tapas style menu and specialises in fresh cheeses. Large building that is shared with artist studio space and perfect for meetings and lunch with clients. Trendy hotspot that is ideal for creative who enjoy networking and post work cocktails.

Wi-Fi: 3/5
Service: 3/5
Atmosphere: 5/5
Power Points: 2/5
Coffee/Menu: 3/5

The Breakfast Club Battersea Rise

(5-9 Battersea Rise, SW11 1HG)

Their breakfast menu is famous and reasonably priced. It's great place to hold meetings, and meet potential clients especially if you are in the creative industries. The décor is funky and spacious, you can over look the street and it’s not too far from the station.

Wi-Fi: 3/5
Service: 5/5
Atmosphere: 4/5
Power Points: 2/5
Coffee/Menu: 5/5

Seven at Brixton

(Unit 7, Market Row, Brixton SW9 8LB)

This is perfect for finishing assignments, and doing long hours online. Mainly Tapas and Cocktails, ideal for a whole day of work, relaxing and calm setting. Try their brunch! Its reasonably priced and popular.

Wi-Fi: 3/5
Service: 5/5
Atmosphere: 2/5
Power Points: 2/5
Coffee/Menu: 3/5

No.67, Camberwell

(67 Peckham Rd, SE5 8UH)

Right next to Camberwell College of Arts, perfect for students who want a chic lunch and lone time on the laptop. Their menu is upscale but reasonably priced. Also it’s easy to get a large space to yourself so ideal for artists.

Wi-Fi: 4/5
Service: 2/5
Atmosphere: 2/5
Power Points: ?/5
Coffee/Menu: 3/5

The Riverfront

(BFI Southbank, Belvedere Road, SE1 8XT)

This is a busy location so not ideal for anyone who requires the quiet. Tourists love the modern décor and convenient location to iconic places in London. Casual mood, so perfect for informal work lunches and ideal if you want some creative inspiration. It's close to the river and reasonably priced.

Wi-Fi: 4/5
Service: 3/5
Atmosphere: 2/5
Power Points: 1/5
Coffee/Menu: 4/5

Federation Coffee

(32 Dover Street, London W1S 4NE)

In the heart of Brixton, very spacious and overlooks the busy high street. Ideal to meet someone for the first time, the mood is casual but you will encounter many freelancers working away. The Coffee is good and everything is made fresh and changes from week to week.

Wi-Fi: 3/5
Service: 3/5
Atmosphere: 2/5
Power Points: 2/5
Coffee/Menu: 3/5

The Lido Café

(Dulwich Rd, Brockwell Lido, SE24 0PA)

Sophisticated setting with amazing brunch menu and calm scenery that overlooks the water. The upscale menu is quite expensive, but the sides are popular, try the anti-pasta.

Wi-Fi: 3/5
Service: 3/5
Atmosphere: 2/5
Power Points: 2/5
Coffee/Menu: 3/5

London Grind

(2 London Bridge, SE1 9RA)

If you need somewhere to stay all day, this is perfect because there are power outlets at every table. The Coffee will keep you going, and the vibe of the whole setting is work orientated with a creative flair. They have a popular espresso bar, and serve brunch, lunch & dinner.

Wi-Fi: 4/5
Service: 3/5
Atmosphere: 3/5
Power Points: 5/5
Coffee/Menu: 3/5

Bar Story

(213 Blenheim Grove, Peckham, SE15 4QL)

Situated behind the station, this is a secret gem. You can get coffee to cocktails, all reasonably priced. Ideal for student and creative people who want to escape the corporate atmosphere of London. It has a communal feel, and a open plan format that is refreshing.

Wi-Fi: 4/5
Service: 3/5
Atmosphere: 4/5
Power Points: 0/5
Coffee/Menu: 3/5

Prince Regent

(69 Dulwich Rd, SE24 ONJ)

Spacious gastro, with outdoor terrace. This is ideal for food and work. The menu is simple and their Sunday roast is the perfect choice for informal meetings. It can also get busy so choose your times wisely.

Wi-Fi: 4/5
Service: 2/5
Atmosphere: 2/5
Power Points: 0/5
